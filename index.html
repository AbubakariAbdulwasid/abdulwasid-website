I am broadly interested in leveraging genomic epidemiology and environmental surveillance to mitigate the spread of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in the Global South. My work focuses on transitioning from cross-sectional snapshots of resistance to longitudinal, data-driven surveillance systems.

By integrating whole-genome sequencing (WGS), phylogeographic modelling with BEAST, and community behavioural assessments, I aim to identify persistent environmental hotspots and develop targeted public-health interventions that break the cycle of environmental transmission.

I am an expert in microbial genomics and bioinformatics (pyseer, BEAST, R, command-line) and I maintain open-source repositories for genomic-assembly pipelines and GWAS data visualisation. I am also a Microbiology Society Champion.

Research interests

What I work on.

Three overlapping threads run through my work — the molecular epidemiology of WHO priority pathogens, environmental & wastewater surveillance for AMR, and computational tooling that makes genomic surveillance possible in low-resource settings.

01 / Molecular epidemiology

ESKAPEE & WHO priority pathogens

Whole-genome epidemiology of Neisseria gonorrhoeae, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and the wider ESKAPEE group, with a focus on plasmid-borne and mutation-driven resistance across West Africa.

02 / One Health surveillance

Wastewater & environmental AMR

Building spatio-temporally indexed biobanks from urban and rural wastewater, and coupling LC-MS/MS antibiotic-residue data with genomic surveillance to map dynamic AMR hotspots in the Ashanti Region.

03 / Computational biology

GWAS & phylogeography

Bacterial GWAS with pyseer on multi-thousand-genome datasets, phylogeographic reconstruction with BEAST, and reproducible R / command-line pipelines for assembly, typing, and lineage assignment.

Pillar 02

Personal Statement, SOP & CV / Resume Review

Structured, line-by-line editorial feedback on the documents that decide most applications: personal statements, statements of purpose, motivation letters, research proposals, and academic CVs.

  • Two review rounds per document
  • Comments on structure, argument, and voice
  • Alignment with scholarship or program criteria
  • Final polish for grammar and formatting

Pillar 03

Bioinformatics & Genomic Surveillance Onboarding

Hands-on introductory mentorship for students entering microbial genomics: assembly pipelines, AMR profiling, GWAS with pyseer, phylogeography with BEAST, and reproducible R workflows.

  • Environment setup (command-line, R, conda)
  • Guided walkthrough on public datasets
  • Code review of a mini-project
  • Reading list and pathway to independence
